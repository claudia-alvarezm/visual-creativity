# Stable diffusion models reveal a persisting human–AI gap in visual creativity

[![Paper](https://img.shields.io/badge/Paper-Advanced%20Science-blue)]() [![Code](https://img.shields.io/badge/Code-R-green)]()

This repository contains the analyses supporting our study comparing visual creativity in human participants and AI image-generation models.

## Associated paper

Rondini, S., Alvarez-Martin, C., Amgermair-Barkai, P., Penacchio, O., Paz, M., Pelowski, M., Dediu, D., Rodriguez-Fornells, A., & Cerda-Company, X. (2026). **Stable diffusion models reveal a persisting human–AI gap in visual creativity.** *Advanced Science* (In Press)

Preprint: [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.16814)

*Abstract*

While recent research suggests Large Language Models match human creative performance in divergent thinking tasks, visual creativity remains underexplored. This study compared image generation in human participants (Visual Artists and Non-Artists) and using an image-generation AI model (two prompting conditions with varying human input: high for Human-Inspired, low for Self-Guided). Human raters (N = 255) and GPT-4o evaluated the creativity of the resulting images. We found a clear creativity gradient: Visual Artists \> Non-Artists ≥ Human-Inspired GenAI \> Self-Guided GenAI. Increased human guidance strongly improved GenAI's creative output, bringing its productions close to those of Non-Artists. Notably, human and AI raters also showed substantially different creativity judgment patterns. These results suggest that, in contrast to language-centered tasks, GenAI models may face unique challenges in visual domains, where creativity depends on perceptual nuance and contextual sensitivity—distinctly human capacities that may not be readily transferable from language models.

## Overview

This project investigates differences in visual creativity between human creators and generative AI systems.

Images were generated by four groups:

-   **Visual Artists**
-   **Non-Artists**
-   **Human-Inspired AI** (AI generation with substantial human prompting)
-   **Self-Guided AI** (AI generation with minimal human input)

Creativity ratings were provided by **255 human raters** and **GPT-4o, with and without human guidance**.\
This repository contains the scripts used to compute creativity scores and reproduce the statistical analyses reported in the paper.

## Repository structure

`scripts/   R Markdown scripts containing the statistical analyses`

`output/    Saved outputs from computationally intensive analyses`

`results/   HTML reports generated from the scripts`

`README.md`

### Scripts

-   **psychometric-structure.Rmd**\
    Contains a detailed analysis of the creativity subscales and how the final creativity score is computed. It also includes comparisons across creator groups and rater types.

-   **visual-creativity-analysis.Rmd**\
    Contains the main statistical analyses presented in the paper, using the creativity score structure derived in the previous script.
